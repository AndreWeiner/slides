partitioned SVD

dealing with very large data

motivation

  • re-use non-MPI SVD
  • flexible resource usage
  • reduce memory constrains
  • use commodity hardware

snapshot data and SVD

$$ \mathbf{X} = \left[\mathbf{x}_1, \mathbf{x}_2,\ldots , \mathbf{x}_N\right] $$

$$ \mathbf{X} = \mathbf{U\Sigma V}^T $$

paritioning in space into $S$ paritions

$$ \mathbf{X} = \left[ \mathbf{X}_1, \mathbf{X}_2, \ldots, \mathbf{X}_S \right]^T $$

(sequential) partitionwise SVD

$$ \mathbf{X}_i = \mathbf{U}_i\mathbf{\Sigma}_i\mathbf{V}^T_i,\quad i \in \left\{ j\in \mathbb{Z} | 1 \leq j \leq S \right\} $$

rearrangement in block matrices

$$ \mathbf{X} = \left[ \mathbf{U}_1\mathbf{\Sigma}_1\mathbf{V}^T_1, \mathbf{U}_2\mathbf{\Sigma}_2\mathbf{V}^T_2, \ldots, \mathbf{U}_S\mathbf{\Sigma}_S\mathbf{V}^T_S \right]^T $$

$$ \mathbf{X} = \mathrm{diag}(\left[ \mathbf{U}_1, \mathbf{U}_2, \ldots, \mathbf{U}_S \right]) \mathbf{Y} $$

$$ \mathbf{Y} = \left[ \mathbf{\Sigma}_1\mathbf{V}^T_1, \mathbf{\Sigma}_2\mathbf{V}^T_2, \ldots, \mathbf{\Sigma}_S\mathbf{V}^T_S \right]^T $$

another small SVD $$ \mathbf{Y}=\mathbf{U}_y\mathbf{\Sigma}_y\mathbf{V}^T_y $$

compare $$ \mathbf{X} = \mathbf{U\Sigma V}^T $$ $$ \mathbf{X} = \mathrm{diag}(\left[ \mathbf{U}_1, \mathbf{U}_2, \ldots, \mathbf{U}_S \right]) \mathbf{U}_y\mathbf{\Sigma}_y\mathbf{V}^T_y $$

comparison requires

$$ \mathbf{U} = \left[\mathbf{U}_1 \mathbf{U}_{y1}, \mathbf{U}_2 \mathbf{U}_{y2}, \ldots, \mathbf{U}_N \mathbf{U}_{yN}\right]^T $$ $$ \mathbf{\Sigma} = \mathbf{\Sigma}_y $$ $$ \mathbf{V} = \mathbf{V}_y $$

sequential evaluation of $\mathbf{U}_i \mathbf{U}_{yi}$

discreate Fourier transform (DFT)

and related methods

DFT of a discretely sampled signal of length $N$

\[ \phi_k = \frac{1}{\sqrt{N}} \sum_{n=0}^{N-1} x_n \, e^{-i 2\pi \frac{k n}{N}}, \ \quad k = 0, 1, \dots, N-1 \]

\[ \mathbf{\Psi}_\mathrm{DFT} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{N}} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 1 & \cdots & 1 \\ 1 & \omega_N & \omega_N^{2} & \cdots & \omega_N^{N-1} \\ 1 & \omega_N^{2} & \omega_N^{4} & \cdots & \omega_N^{2(N-1)} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ 1 & \omega_N^{N-1} & \omega_N^{2(N-1)} & \cdots & \omega_N^{(N-1)^2} \end{bmatrix} \]

with $ \omega_N = e^{-\,i\,2\pi / N} $

projection of data matrix onto $\mathbf{\Psi}_\mathrm{DFT}$

\[ \hat{\mathbf{\Phi}} = \mathbf{X} \mathbf{\Psi}_\mathrm{DFT} \]

normalization to unit length

\[ \mathbf{\phi}_i = \hat{\mathbf{\phi}}_i / |\hat{\mathbf{\phi}}_i|,\quad b_i = |\hat{\mathbf{\phi}}_i| \]

final modal representation

\[ \mathbf{X} = \mathbf{\Phi} \mathbf{D}_\mathbf{b} \mathbf{\Psi}_\mathrm{DFT},\ \mathbf{D}_\mathbf{b} = \mathrm{diag}(\mathbf{b}) \]

computational trick (economy SVD)

\[ \mathbf{X} = \mathbf{U\Sigma V}^T, \quad \tilde{\mathbf{X}} = \mathbf{U}^T \mathbf{X} = \mathbf{\Sigma V}^T \]

DFT of $\tilde{\mathbf{X}}$

\[ \tilde{\mathbf{X}} = \tilde{\mathbf{\Phi}} \tilde{\mathbf{D}}_\mathbf{b} \mathbf{\Psi}_\mathrm{DFT} \]

projection onto $\mathbf{U}$

\[ \mathbf{X} = \mathbf{U}\tilde{\mathbf{X}} = \mathbf{U} \tilde{\mathbf{\Phi}} \tilde{\mathbf{D}}_\mathbf{b} \mathbf{\Psi}_\mathrm{DFT} \]

comparison with direct DFT

\[ \mathbf{X} = \mathbf{U} \tilde{\mathbf{\Phi}} \tilde{\mathbf{D}}_\mathbf{b} \mathbf{\Psi}_\mathrm{DFT} \] \[ \mathbf{X} = \mathbf{\Phi} \mathbf{D}_\mathbf{b} \mathbf{\Psi}_\mathrm{DFT} \]

note that $|\mathbf{U}\tilde{\mathbf{\phi}}_i| = |\tilde{\mathbf{\phi}}_i| = 1$

\[ \rightarrow \tilde{\mathbf{D}}_\mathbf{b} = \mathbf{D}_\mathbf{b},\ \mathbf{\Phi} = \mathbf{U} \tilde{\mathbf{\Phi}} \]

some properties

  • rank truncation possible (noise reduction)
  • only one hyperparameter ($r$)
  • DFT of $r$ instead of $M$ observables
  • individual mode reconstruction
  • applicable to Welch or SPOD
